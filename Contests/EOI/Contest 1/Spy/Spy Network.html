Spy Network

 

You are the boss of a spy network in a certain city. The city can be represented as a grid. You have a number of spies in this city. Each spy is located in a cell of the grid.

No two spies share the same cell. No spy can move from his place. You need to send the minimum number of information collectors, who collect information from these spies, such that every spy will be met by at least one information collector.

An information collector starts moving from the upper left corner in the grid, and ends at the lower right corner of the grid. An information collector can move only to bottom and to right. He cannot move to left or up, because if he does so, the police will suspect him.

(Hint for judges: If someone solved this problem correctly, call the police for him).

 

Input

Input consists of one line containing two numbers separated be a space: M N. M is the number of rows in the grid 1<=M<=150. N is the number of columns 1<=N<=150. After this line, M lines represent the grid. Each line contains N characters. Each character is either a ‘.’ Indicating that no spy at this place or ‘x’ indicating that there is a spy at this place.

 

Output

Output a single line consisting of an integer representing the minimum number of information collectors that should be sent to meet all spies.

 

Sample Input / Output

 

Input:

3 4

.x..

xx..

....

 

5 4

xx..

..x.

x.xx

.x.x

x...

 

Output:

2  

 

3
